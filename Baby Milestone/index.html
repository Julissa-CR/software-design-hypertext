Baby Milestone
  • 0-3 months
  • 4-6 months
  • 7-9 months
  • 10-12 months

0-3 months

Key Milestones

  • Baby may now smile
  • Baby is learning to make cooing sounds
  • Baby may start to lift their head

Newborn

  • Newborn sleep around 16 hours a day

1 month

  • Be sure to give baby tummy time
  • May spit up, but isnâ€™t a concern
  • Baby massage can stimulate your baby sense of touch

2 month

  • Baby may discover hands and feet
  • Baby may begin to lift head at about a 45 degree angle

3 month

  • Use toys that make noise, light up or play music
  • Baby may begin to lift head at about a 45 to 90 degree angle

4-6 months

Key Milestones

  • Baby will begin to smile at themselves in the mirror and may respond differently to the voices of a stranger versus someone familiar
  • Baby is imitating your facial expression and may begin using vowel sounds.
  • Baby will be able to hold on their head up on the own. They may also begin to roll over.

4 month

  • Will be able to turn to the noise of a sound
  • By now should be able to lift head by 90 degree
  • Should be able to roll over at least one way; typically belly to back

5 month

  • Hearing and vision are almost completely developed
  • Baby will explore everything within reach and putting in my; including their own feet
  • Baby can raise up on their arms during tummy time and have head control when sitting up.

6 month

  • Can begin eating solids
  • May recognize their own name
  • May get up on all fours, and begin sitting unassisted

7-9 months

Key Milestones

  • Baby begins to pick up on feelings and tone in your voice
  • Baby is now babbling, blowing bubbles and practicing speech. They may even respond to their name.
  • Baby is learning to sit by themselves.

7 month

  • Baby is begining to develop emotions
  • Memory is developing rapidly
  • Potential new moves: spinning; dragging; scooting and rocking

8 month

  • Baby will soon be ready for finger food
  • Baby is on the move and may begin standing
  • Exploring with a purpose; begins to makes sense of cause and effect

9 month

  • Can begin to pull up and cruise
  • Big advance in cognition and memory
  • Baby may communicate through pointing, gesturing, grunting, and facial expression

10-12 months

Key Milestones

  • Baby may have seperation anxiety and be hesistant around starnagers
  • Baby may start to point as a way to communicate with you.
  • Baby is crawling, however may start pulling themesleves up and stand with help. They may even start to walk.

10 month

  • Baby may begin walking unassisted
  • May clap their hands; wave good bye, blow kisses
  • Baby may recognize words and phrases and anticipate familiar routines

11 month

  • Baby is more mobil and independent
  • Baby is ready for play dates and parallel play
  • Baby may say a word or two, however main form of communication is pointing and gesturing

12 month

  • Officially a toddler
  • Can start to reduce bottle feeding
  • Baby is potentially walking and talking
